YUFEI LIU

I am a undergraduate student from Wellesley College double majoring in Psychology and Cinema and Media Studies. I am an undergraduate research assistant at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. I previously worked at MIT Language Lab and now I work at MIT STEP Education Education Arcade where I design interactive educational games for students. Currently, we are partnering with DC Public Schools to redesign high school students' STEM course material. Using a 3D agent-based computational modeling game StarLogo Nova, our aim is to design fun, helpful, and accessible games for students to learn better and faster in classroom settings.

Starting fall 2023, I will be attending Harvard University Graduate School of Education to pursue Master of Education degree in Learning Design, Innovation, and Technology with a focus on STEM games design. I am very passionate about bringing changes into the classrooms as a student and a media designer.

Coming from an international background, I am proud to call Beijing, China my hometown, and I have also lived in Los Angeles, Shanghai, Oxford, and Boston. In my free time, I love watching movies, exploring new recipes, ice skating, and learning new languages. I have a cute mini-schunauzer back at home, his name is Ricky!

Research

MIT Scheller Teacher Education Program Education Arcade

I am a curriculum designer at MIT STEP Education Arcade. We are currently working on DC models where we redesign public high school STEM courses using StarLogo Nova, a 3D agent-based simulator game which also allows students to build computational modeling skills as they play.

MIT Language Lab (TedLab)

I am a undergraduate researcher at MIT Language Lab. My team studied individual's language experiences on legal documents and whether or not their abilities to recall and comprehend these legal propositions are affected by their experiences. Our peer-reviewed journal article was published to The Cognitive Science Society in May 2021.

Wellesley College Psychology Research

My Psychology senior capstone research project focused on studying the relationship between lying skills and lie detection skills. I was interested in whether the two skills are positively or negatively related. My other Personality Psychology research studies the relationship between personal narrative identity and group identities.
